## ADDED Requirements

### Requirement: Simulator projects future review load under a hypothetical add rate
The system SHALL expose a `simulate_review_forecast(new_cards_per_day, horizon_days, graduating_interval_days)` command. It SHALL combine the real due-workload forecast (from `get_due_workload_forecast`) with a projection of reviews generated by adding `new_cards_per_day` new cards each day, where each new card spawns follow-up reviews at intervals of `graduating_interval_days`, then multiplied by 2.5 for each subsequent "Good" review, clamped to the horizon.

#### Scenario: Adding 10 cards/day over 30 days adds projected re-reviews
- **WHEN** `simulate_review_forecast(10.0, 30, 1.0)` is called
- **THEN** each day's point SHALL include `baseline_due` (real forecast) plus `added_reviews` (projected reviews from the hypothetical new cards whose intervals fall on that day)

#### Scenario: Zero add rate returns the baseline unchanged
- **WHEN** `simulate_review_forecast(0.0, 30, 1.0)` is called
- **THEN** every point's `added_reviews` SHALL be 0 and `simulated_due` SHALL equal `baseline_due`

### Requirement: Simulator returns aggregate metadata
The response SHALL include `total_baseline` (sum of baseline due across horizon), `total_simulated` (sum of baseline + added), `total_new_reviews` (sum of added), `peak_day` (date string of the highest-`simulated_due` day), and `peak_count` (that day's `simulated_due`).

#### Scenario: Peak day is reported
- **WHEN** the simulator runs over a horizon where day 22 has the highest simulated load
- **THEN** `peak_day` SHALL be day 22's date and `peak_count` SHALL equal that day's `simulated_due`

### Requirement: Simulator never mutates persistent data
The simulator SHALL be read-only. It SHALL NOT insert, update, or delete any learning items, review results, or scheduling fields.

#### Scenario: Running the simulator has no side effects
- **WHEN** `simulate_review_forecast` is called
- **THEN** no rows in `learning_items`, `review_results`, or `documents` SHALL be modified
